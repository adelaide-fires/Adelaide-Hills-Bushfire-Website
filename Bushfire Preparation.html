The Importance of Preparation:

    A well-prepared home is more likely to survive a bushfire
    Preparations assist in protecting not only your home but also your neighbours'
    Even if you plan to leave, a prepared home has a higher chance of survival

To Do List:

Home Preparation:

Use non-flammable building materials
Clear vegetation and rubbish from around your home
Seal gaps in rooves, walls and floors to prevent ember entry
Install metal fly wire mesh or solid screens on windows, doors, ventilators, and skylights
Ensure home and contents insurance coverage

Garden Preparation:

Before the Fire Season:

Remove dead branches, leaves and undergrowth
Prune tree limbs lower than 2 metres or those overhanging the home
Manage vegetation within 20 metres of home and 5 metres from sheds
Clear bark, mulch, wood piles and other flammables close to structures

Other Precautions:

Set up a sprinkler system
Ensure a water supply of at least 5000 litres
Install a petrol/diesel-driven water pump
Ensure hoses are long enough to reach the entirety of your home
Plant low flammability vegetation and spaced trees

Seasonal Measures:

Spring:

Slash/mow grass
Remove fallen debris
Service mechanical equipment
Update your Bushfire Survival Plan


Summer:

Maintain a defendable space around your home
Monitor Fire Danger Ratings



Autumn:

Cleanup post-summer
Check for burnoff permits




Winter:

Clear gutters
Remove dead vegetation
Prune tree limbs



Other Preparation Strategies:

Create an Emergency Evacuation Kit:

Essential documents and backups on a USB stick
Battery-operated radio and torch with extra batteries
Protective clothing, P2 masks and woollen blankets
Map with marked routes
Emergency contact list
Adequate water, food and first aid kit
Special considerations for pets


Stay Updated:

  • Understand the Fire Danger Rating system
  • Familiarise yourself with different levels of Bushfire Warnings
  • Subscribe to CFS emails for updates
  • Listen to Emergency Broadcasts
  • Use the AlertSA app and follow social media updates
  • Call the Information Hotline 1800 362 361 for more information and advice
