Fire Danger Ratings

No Rating:

  • No action is required

Moderate:

  • Plan, prepare and stay informed about fires
  • Most fires that occur in this rating can be controlled
  • Be ready to act in the event of a fire

High:

  • Be prepared and remain vigilant
  • Fires can pose a danger, so stay updated and make decisions on actions you may wish to take
  • If fires ignite, they could endanger lives and properties and individuals should avoid high-risk zones

Extreme:

  • Continuously monitor official channels and take preemptive actions
  • Immediate action in the event of a fire is essential
  • Staying is only advisable if adequately prepared, otherwise, consider avoiding or leaving these zones

Catastrophic:

  • For safety, evacuating bushfire risk areas is advised
  • Fires under this rating can lead to significant loss of life and property, as many homes aren't designed for such conditions
  • Avoiding travel in high-risk zones is very important, as fires can cause extensive damage

Restrictions during the Fire Danger Season

Not Permitted:

  • Fires without a permit
  • Burn offs without a permit
  • Burning rubbish without a permit

Are Permitted:

  • Gas or electric barbeques, pizza ovens and other cooking appliances, as long as they are away from vegetation and the appropriate extinguisher is nearby
  • Kettle barbeques, as long as they are away from vegetation and the appropriate extinguisher is nearby
  • Wood fired pizza ovens must comply with these codes of compliance in order to be allowed

For more information, visit the CFS What can I do / What can't I do? page.
